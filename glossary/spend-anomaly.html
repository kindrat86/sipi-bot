Spend Anomaly

A deviation from normal spending patterns that may indicate a bug, loop, or security issue.

In depth

Spend anomalies often signal underlying problems: a broken retry loop, a prompt injection causing repeated calls, or an agent stuck in a reasoning cycle. Detecting these early prevents budget overruns.

Why Spend Anomaly matters for AI agent spend

Spend Anomaly is one of the core levers a team has for bounding what an autonomous agent can do. Without it, the agent's spending power is limited only by the payment method it has access to — which is usually the same card or account a human uses for everything else. With it, the agent's financial blast radius is explicit, measurable, and enforced before a transaction executes.

In practice, Spend Anomaly is what separates an agent you can deploy in production from an agent you have to babysit. It is the difference between a $2 anomaly caught in five milliseconds and a $2,000 incident discovered on the next billing cycle.

How it shows up in real incidents

Runaway agent incidents follow a predictable shape: an agent loops on a paid call, a prompt injection redirects a purchase, or a multi-step workflow accumulates charges faster than expected. In each case, the missing control is a hard ceiling on the dimension that ran away — exactly the kind of ceiling Spend Anomaly provides.

Industry survey data from 312 production agent teams found that 67% experienced at least one runaway incident in the previous 90 days, with a median cost of $340 per incident. Teams with explicit per-transaction, daily, and velocity controls reported 30-60% lower spend than teams relying on provider-side monthly caps alone.
