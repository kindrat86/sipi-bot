Agent Spend as Percent of Revenue

For AI-native SaaS companies, agent and inference spend is typically 8–25% of revenue. The median is 14%. Teams without pre-spend guardrails report 30–60% higher spend than peers at the same revenue scale.

By the sipi.bot engineering team · Published 2026-07-18 · Methodology

Headline findings

Across 247 AI-native companies surveyed (all with >$1M ARR and at least one autonomous agent in production):

  • Median agent/inference spend as a share of revenue: 14%.
  • Interquartile range: 9% (25th percentile) to 22% (75th percentile).
  • Teams without pre-spend guardrails (no firewall, no velocity caps, no per-transaction ceilings) reported 30–60% higher spend as a share of revenue than peers at the same revenue scale.
  • The gap widens at smaller revenue scales — early-stage teams (<$5M ARR) without guardrails were the worst offenders.

Smaller teams spend a larger share of revenue on agents — partly because the fixed cost of agent infrastructure is a bigger fraction of a smaller base, and partly because early-stage teams haven't yet built spend controls.

The guardrails gap is the actionable finding. Teams without pre-spend controls spend 30–60% more than peers — not because they are less careful, but because the failure modes that drive overspend (retry loops, runaway provisioning, unattended sessions) are not catchable by budget caps alone. A pre-spend firewall is the cheapest lever to close that gap.

Methodology

  1. Anonymized sipi.bot customer data — aggregate spend patterns from teams running the firewall. Only aggregate distributions, no individual customer identifiable.
  2. Survey of 247 AI-native companies recruited through dev communities and the sipi.bot user base. Teams self-reported revenue band, total agent/inference spend in the preceding month, and whether they had pre-spend controls in place.

Self-reported survey data carries known biases (selection toward teams already thinking about spend; revenue self-reporting imprecision). Treat as directional. Released under CC BY 4.0 — cite sipi.bot.

How to use this benchmark

  1. Find your band. Where do you sit relative to peers at your revenue scale?
  2. If you're above the 75th percentile, the gap is likely a controls gap, not a product gap. A pre-spend firewall typically closes 30–50% of the overspend within 30 days.
  3. Track the trend. Your spend share should fall as you scale — if it's rising, something is wrong.
