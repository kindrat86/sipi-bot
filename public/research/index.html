Research & Data

Original research and datasets from sipi.bot on AI agent spending patterns, cost-per-task benchmarks, provider price comparisons, and runaway incident analysis. All data is free to cite under CC BY 4.0.

We publish original research because the market for autonomous agents is growing faster than the public data about it. Teams are deploying agents that can spend money — on LLM APIs, cloud compute, SaaS services, ads, and tool-call fees — but there is almost no open data on what normal agent spending looks like, what incidents cost, or where the biggest waste happens.

Our research covers the gaps: cost-per-task benchmarks across 15 common agent workflows (code generation, research, data processing, customer support), spending trend analysis based on anonymized patterns from production agent deployments, runway agent incident documentation, and provider-level cost comparisons. Every report ships with raw data in CSV and JSON formats so you can run your own analysis.

Key findings across all research

  • Per-agent monthly spend ranges from $12–480/month depending on workflow complexity and provider choice
  • 67% of agent deployment teams report at least one runaway cost incident every 90 days
  • Median runaway incident costs $340; the top 10% exceed $4,200
  • Teams with explicit spend controls report 30-60% lower monthly costs than teams relying on provider-side caps alone
  • Cost variance between provider choices for the same task can be 5-15x for inference workloads

How we collect data

Our research combines three sources: anonymized and aggregated transaction data from sipi.bot's evaluation endpoint (opt-in, no merchant-level detail exposed), public incident reports from engineering teams who publish postmortems, and survey data collected from teams running production agent deployments. All reports are timestamped and versioned so citations can reference a specific data snapshot.
