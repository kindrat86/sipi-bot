How the score is calculated

The risk score is a 0–100 sum of five weighted factors, computed entirely in your browser: budget exposure (up to 30 points, from how far your worst-case month overshoots your budget), unsupervised 24/7 operation (20 points), multi-agent compounding (15 points), missing guardrails (25 points if you have none, 15 if you only have a hardcoded check), and high transaction frequency (up to 10 points). 70 or above is rated critical, 40–69 elevated.

The worst-case projection models the failure mode that actually empties budgets: a runaway retry loop. It assumes a 4× transaction burst, capped at 2,000 transactions per day, sustained across your unsupervised hours — 24 per day if your agent runs around the clock, 10 if it works business hours. Your inputs never leave the page; nothing you type is sent to a server.

The recommended rules map one-to-one onto sipi.bot's rule types — per-transaction limit, daily total cap, velocity limit, time window, and approval threshold — so you can paste them straight into the dashboard rule editor or test them first in the live playground.
