How much are companies spending on AI APIs in 2026

Bottom Line AI API spending varies dramatically in 2026: startups spend $500-5,000/month, mid-market companies $5,000-50,000/month, and enterprises $50,000-500,000+/month — with spending growing 15-25% month-over-month for companies without spending controls.

Spending by Company Size

Seed-stage startups: $500-2,000/month. Series A: $2,000-10,000/month. Series B+: $10,000-50,000/month. Enterprise: $50,000-500,000+/month. These are medians — AI-native companies (building on top of LLMs) spend 3-5x more than AI-adjacent companies.

Growth Rate Without Controls

Companies without API spending controls see 15-25% month-over-month cost growth — doubling every 3-4 months. With spending controls (limits, monitoring, routing), growth slows to 5-10% month-over-month while maintaining output quality.

Cost Per Use Case

Chatbot/customer service: $0.01-0.10 per interaction. Code generation: $0.10-1.00 per task. Document analysis: $0.50-5.00 per document. Agentic workflows (multi-step): $1.00-20.00 per task. Costs vary 10x depending on model choice and prompt efficiency.

The Hidden Infrastructure Cost

API costs are only 30-40% of total AI spending. The rest goes to: prompt engineering and management (20%), evaluation and testing (15%), monitoring and observability (15%), and compliance/governance (10-15%). Full AI cost management must account for all of these.

Key Data Points

  • $500-500K/month spending range
  • 15-25% monthly growth without controls
  • 30-40% of AI spend is API costs

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How do I benchmark my AI spending?

    Compare your cost-per-task and cost-per-user against your industry peers. If you're spending 2x the median for your company size without proportional output, you likely have optimization opportunities in model routing, prompt efficiency, or usage patterns.

Key considerations for answers

  • Enforcement vs. observation: Dashboards tell you what happened. A spend firewall stops what should not happen. Both are useful; they are not substitutes. Start with enforcement, add observation for attribution and analytics.
  • Per-agent vs. global policies: Different agents have different spend profiles. A research agent and a billing agent need different limits. Configure per-agent policies from day one.
  • Velocity limits are non-negotiable: A per-transaction limit will not catch a loop of 200 small calls. A velocity cap (max transactions per minute) is the specific control for the most common runaway pattern.
  • Merchant allowlists prevent the worst-case scenario: A compromised agent can be redirected to any destination. An allowlist ensures it can only transact with vendors you have explicitly approved.
